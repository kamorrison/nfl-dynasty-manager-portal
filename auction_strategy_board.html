Three Auction Plans

Stars and Scrubs

Pay for one or two premium difference-makers, then attack low-cost upside later.

Budget allocation: Top $70 | Mid $38 | Depth $20

Top targets: None

When to pivot: Pivot if your top targets go 15 percent above plan max bids.

Risk: Missing on your first two swings can leave too much money chasing weak depth.

Upside: Highest chance to leave with a true anchor player.

Best if: Elite RB/WR prices are attainable and Killanomics can win one early bidding war.

Balanced Build

Split money across multiple starting-caliber targets and stay flexible through the room.

Budget allocation: Top $45 | Mid $58 | Depth $25

Top targets: None

When to pivot: Pivot if your main need tier dries up before you buy two starters.

Risk: You may lose top-end upside if elite names come in below expectation.

Upside: Most stable way to fill multiple starting needs without panic spending.

Best if: The room prices stars correctly and several WR/RB/TE options stay clustered.

Value/Depth Build

Let the room overspend early, then accumulate useful starters and bench leverage in the middle tiers.

Budget allocation: Top $26 | Mid $58 | Depth $44

Top targets: None

When to pivot: Pivot if the room stays too slow and your value windows never open.

Risk: If the room stays disciplined, your patient approach can miss the only true league-winners.

Upside: Best path to roster breadth and late-room leverage.

Best if: Managers with larger budgets overspend in the opening tiers.
